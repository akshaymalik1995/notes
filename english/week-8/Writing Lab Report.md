## Writing Lab Reports

All projects, all experiments in all laboratories have two kinds of writing: 
- One before, what is sought out to be done and how it is done or how it can be done, that is called **manual**.
- Second after, What was done? How it was done? Whether the project became successful ? Whether the experiment provided the result that was expected or whether it gave a different result? Or it failed simply; it must be recorded so that other researchers, other workers in the laboratory learn from these experiments.

### How are reports written ?

All experiments in all laboratories are followed by **reports**.

These reports generally answer the following questions:
- What was done?
- Why it was done?
- How it was done?
    1. Tools
    2. Methods
    3. Materials
    4. Process
    5. Any other relevant information

Making a tea is also an experimental project in a lab.

**A REPORT ON MAKING TEA**

1. _What was done_ : Green Tea was made for one.
2. _Why it was done_ : To welcome a visitor, as a mark of hospitality.
3. _How it was done_ - 
- **Tools** : Percolator, Cup and Saucer, Spoon, Unbroken Tea Leaves (Half a teaspoon), Honey, Strainer
- **Methods**: 
    1. A cup and a quarter of water was poured into the percolator.
    2. Percolator was switched on and time noted.
    3. 40 seconds later water started boiling.
    4. Percolator, on thermostat, switched itself off after 40 seconds.
    5. Meanwhile, teapot made of ceramic clay was warmed for 30 seconds in a micro-oven.
    6. Micro-oven was taken out.
    7. Half a teaspoonful of tea leaves were kept in the pot.
    8. Boiled water was gently poured from near the mouth of the pot so as not to break the leaves.
    9. The leaves first surfaced above the water.
    10. Mouth of the teapot was closed with its lid.
    11. Meanwhile, cup and saucer was warmed in a micro-oven for 30 seconds.
    12. Strain them as you pour.
    13. Cup was kept on the saucer.
    14. After three minutes, lid was taken off the teapot. It was observed that the leaves had soaked as much water as they could, and had got settled at the bottom in the pot.
    15. The colour of the water had now changed to greenish-yellow.
    16. Boiled water was poured into the cup.
    17. One teaspoon full of honey was added to the cup and the same was stirred with a teaspoon.
    18. Cup was put on the saucer.
    19. Saucer was held by the rim and offered to the guest.

- **Materials:** 
    1. Tea leaves (Half a teaspoon)
    2. Honey- One teaspoon
    3. Water- 77.93 ML

- **Process** - The process involved the following:
    1. Warming of solid objects
    2. Boiling of liquids
    3. Soaking of one substance by another
    4. Stirring

- **Any other relevent information**:
    This is not a new experiment, but a demonstration will prove that best tea can be got even today only with the traditional material in a traditional way.
